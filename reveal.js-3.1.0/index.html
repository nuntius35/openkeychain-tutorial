Step 0: What you need to know about Cryptography

  • Modern cryptography is based on advanced mathematics,
  • such as prime numbers, logarithms and elliptic curves.
  • Luckily it works even if you don't understand it.
  • However, it helps to know some basics.

Asymmetric cryptography

  • PGP encryption for email is asymmetric: all keys come in pairs (public and private).
  • A public key is used to encrypt a message.
  • Only the owner of the associated private key can decrypt such a message.
  • Since he is the only one who knows the private key he can prove his identity: sign a message.
  • Carefully remember the rules on the next slide.

The rules

  • Your own public key is not a secret.
  • On the opposite: Upload it to a keyserver and let the world know about it.
  • If you want to send someone a secret message, you need to obtain the persons's public key.
  • Your private key is secret.
  • Never ever show it to anyone. Protect it with a strong password.
  • If you want to read a message sent to you, you need access to your private key.
  • Hence, if you loose your private key, you cannot read messages sent to you. Make a backup.
